# Afoi Deli — Operating Doctrine

> [!quote]

## The core asset: the uplift engine

Afoi Deli's moat is **not its brand portfolio.** The portfolio is interchangeable inputs. The moat is the **uplift** — the capacity to make any material it touches read as more premium, more certain, more desirable than it is anywhere else.

> [!success] The direction of value
> Value flows **from Afoi Deli to the brand**, not the other way around. A lesser brand staged in the showroom, placed in the family aesthetic, and sold by the family's people becomes more premium than it is on its own. The brands are not lucky to be carried — they are *elevated* by being carried.

This is why everyone wants Afoi Deli as a client *regardless of numbers*. The scale and the taste are the scarce asset. The brands are abundant; the engine is not.

**Implication:** the brands that would hurt to lose are the **identity brands** — the ones that *are* Afoi Deli's character ([[Supplier - Kronos|Kronos]], Cielo, [[Supplier - Mutina|Mutina]], the heavy aesthetic houses). But even these are not load-bearing the way the engine is. The opposition can access the same brands; they cannot access the same uplift.

## No-promise selling

The opposition deals in numbers and sales promises to the brands. **Afoi Deli never promises sales to anyone.**

- Driven by what the client actually needs and likes.
- Genuine taste expressed when sampling — you show what you'd choose.
- Lean on technical certainty as honest salespeople, not on forecasts.

This is [[The Heart|"create the circumstances, don't ask"]] applied to the sales floor. You don't tell a brand "I'll move X units." You build the conditions under which the client wants the thing, and the sales follow as consequence.

## The responsibility doctrine

> [!important] Where the buck stops
> When problems arise, the client turns toward **Afoi Deli** — not the representative, not even the brand. The brand has a duty to support; the representative has a duty to coordinate. But final accountability sits with Afoi Deli, always, regardless of whose fault it is.

The client buys the brand; the client *trusts you*. You absorb the accountability that the brand and the representative can partially deflect. This is [[The Heart|"value is an obligation"]] applied to the firm. It is the burden of being the merchant at the end of the chain — and it is also exactly why the uplift works. Afoi Deli is what makes the brand mean something in Greece.

## Representation models

How a brand reaches you tells you how much leverage and friction sits behind it. Encode this as a `representation_model` field on each supplier note.

| Model | Description | Leverage |
|---|---|---|
| `independent_office` | Independent rep takes a **% of sales as their income**, carries a portfolio, acts as *mediator*. Most brands. Structurally **replaceable** — they need you more than you need them. | High (yours) |
| `salaried_agent` | Brand **employs** a local agent on salary; the % of sales is *not* the agent's personal income. Agent represents the brand's interest, not their own commission. (e.g. Atlas Concorde, [[Supplier - Florim|Florim]], Marazzi tier.) | Mixed |
| `coordinator_pr` | The most advanced brands: agent as **coordinator and PR, not gatekeeper**. Effectively a near-direct relationship with the factory; the agent smooths rather than stands between. | High (shared) |
| `direct` | Direct factory relationship. The target end-state of the evolved mercantile model. | Highest |

> [!tip] The strategic tell
> When an agent stops being a gatekeeper and becomes a facilitator (`coordinator_pr`), you are effectively dealing with the factory directly. This is the direction Afoi Deli is moving — toward direct factory capacity.

**On representatives generally:** a relationship that works does not need to be meddled with. Stay warm in practice; file the truth that the conduit is replaceable. What matters is the brand and the client, not the conduit. (See [[Supplier - Kronos|Kronos]] / Plus Interiors–Tzanidis for the worked example, including the founding Cielo hint.)

## The family as commercial instruments

The owners are not interchangeable. Each is a distinct force in the engine. (Roles only here; the people live in [[People and Roles Map]] and, at the root, in [[The Heart]].)

- **[[People and Roles Map|Kostas]] (father)** — the maxims, the merchant's nerve, the almost otherworldly intuition for what is coming. The strategist and founder.
- **[[People and Roles Map|Chrysoula]] (mother)** — the closer. The human gift that turns a staged showroom into a signed sale. *"Can sell ice to Eskimos."*
- **[[People and Roles Map|Orfeas]] (you)** — the successor, the steward. Holds the whole at a standard and carries the doctrine forward.
- **[[People and Roles Map|Ektoras]] (brother)** — *(to be described — room left open).*

The engine has two halves: the **staging** (taste, discretion, aesthetic — the showroom that makes a brand look expensive) and the **conversion** (the human gift that makes the client say yes). The uplift needs both. The conversion half — how a general brief becomes a specified, sold scheme at the sample wall — is documented in [[The Selection Engine]].

## A known pattern to watch

> [!warning] Strength and vulnerability, same trait
> The family level-headedness — the discretion, the composure, never demanding — is the moat in one frame and a liability in another. More aggressive competitors and some counterparties **misread it as softness.** It is not. But be aware it can be misread, and that the misreading occasionally needs a quiet, firm reality check. Friends up to a point; commercial past it.
