## Identity — "Timeless Nature"

Pierre Vive (French, *"living stone"*) is Kronos's homage to natural stone — *"living through time, able to renew itself through the fresh eyes of contemporary design."* It reproduces the authentic materiality of quarried stone, deliberately keeping the **imperfections of wear** — the shades, variations and flaws *"as integral to its value."* The textures evoke the work of **traditional masons**: a hand-made look that reads as genuinely natural rather than printed. It is positioned as the **newest** stone line in the Kronos range (per [[Kronos — Collections Reference]]; Kronos's own channels announce it as a *New Collection* and the catalogue carries a 2025 edition date — the catalogue text itself makes no recency claim), and sits at the heart of the house's "research and memory, innovation and material origins" register.

## The range — colourways × graphic versions

**Four colourways** (`varianti cromatiche`), running cool-pale → warm → dark:

| Colourway | Tone | Reads as |
|---|---|---|
| **Loire** | palest, cool greige / limestone-white | light, serene, Nordic-leaning stone |
| **Orval** | warm sand / beige | sunlit limestone, Mediterranean |
| **Morvan** | warm taupe / light brown | travertine warmth |
| **Brionne** | deep espresso-brown | dark, grounding, dramatic stone |

**Two graphic versions** (`versioni grafiche`) — the same colour, two "ages" of the stone face:

- **Noble** — the cleaner, more compact and uniform face; contemporary, calmer movement.
- **Ancienne** — the weathered, time-worn face with stronger mineral mottling and surface "imperfections"; the aged-stone look.
- **Noble Trace** — a veined/striated graphic of the Noble face (used in the larger formats and the Trace Mix mosaic).
- **Ancienne Vintage** — a multi-format, mixed-size **laying programme** (Modulo 120 / Modulo 80, with vintage bordo) that recreates an old-floor, quarry-cut composition.

## Surfaces, formats & thicknesses

> [!note] Three independent axes: **colour** (4) × **graphic version** (Noble / Ancienne) × **surface** (standard / Grip).
> Every colour/graphic comes in a **standard** (un-suffixed, matte-structured) surface and a **Grip** (anti-slip) surface; the 20 mm tier is the **2.0 / SKE 2.0** raised-outdoor system (always grip-class). Edges are **rectified** as standard; a **Bordo Vintage** (aged) edge is offered for the Ancienne Vintage laying.

| Surface | Normalized | Where it's used |
|---|---|---|
| Standard (un-suffixed) ¹ | `matte` + `structured` | indoor floors & walls, the default |
| **Grip** (`finishes_raw`) | `anti-slip` | wet areas, outdoor, the 2.0 tier |

¹ The catalogue names **only** the *Grip* surface; the default surface is shown by bare colour name (e.g. "Loire"), not by a surface word. "Standard / matte-structured" is our normalization, read from the stone texture in the swatch images — hence only `Grip` sits in `finishes_raw` (the verbatim brand term). The **Noble / Ancienne / Trace / Vintage** distinctions are *graphic versions*, not surfaces → captured in `graphic_versions` (see the range section above).

## Per-surface properties matrix

The inspiration catalogue states no performance figures, and Pierre Vive publishes **no line-stamped technical sheet**. But its Grip and 20 mm surfaces *are* the Kronos **SKE 2.0** system, whose certified technical table **is** published — so the values below are **`verified` for SKE 2.0** and **`likely` for Pierre Vive** (inherited from the shared system). For a client certificate, request a line-stamped `scheda tecnica` from Kronos.

| Surface | DIN 51130 (R) | DIN 51097 (A/B/C) | DCOF (wet) | Deep abrasion | Frost | Fire |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Naturale (standard) | R10 | A | > 0.42 | 175 mm³ | PASS | A1 / A1fl |
| **Grip** | R11 | A + B + C | > 0.42 | < 128 mm³ | PASS | A1 / A1fl |
| **2.0** (20 mm outdoor) | R11 | A + B + C | > 0.42 | < 128 mm³ | PASS | A1 / A1fl |

- **Water absorption** Eᵥ ≤ 0.5 % (BIa class guarantee); Kronos publishes **≤ 0.04 %** company-wide. **Bending strength** declared **53 N/mm²** (EN ISO 10545-4; min req 35). **20 mm slab breaking load** EN 1339 **> 14 kN** (≈ 1400 kg/slab) — rated for collective/public use, medium-heavy traffic.
- **PEI** is not published: the 20 mm outdoor tier is full-body unglazed porcelain (rated by deep abrasion, not PEI); a PEI for the glazed indoor 9 mm faces is unstated → `needs_check` if a client asks.
- *Source:* Kronos **SKE 2.0** outdoor catalogue technical table (EN ISO 10545 / EN 1339 / EN 13501-1) — see Sources. Confidence: **verified** for SKE 2.0, **likely** for Pierre Vive (shared system).

## Applications

- **Indoor** floors & walls — all colourways, both graphics (the 120×280 slab is the statement wall).
- **Outdoor** — the **20 mm 2.0 / SKE 2.0** grip tier: terraces, garden paths, the **outdoor kitchen** staged in Morvan (catalogue pp. 28–29). Pairs with the outdoor-living / Mediterranean-villa segment Afoi Deli targets.
- **K-LAB potential** — as a Kronos line it can feed [[Supplier - Kronos|K-LAB]] bespoke pieces: the catalogue shows a Pierre Vive **washbasin**, **bench** and **niche** cut from the slab.

## Aesthetic, mood & narrative

> [!quote] The brand's own register (raw — preserve)
> *"A homage to natural stone: living through time… the authentic texture of stone… its imperfections that reflect its wear over time, revealing its shades, variations and flaws as integral to its value… the work of traditional masons: a hand-made look."* — Kronos, *Pierre Vive / Timeless Nature*

The story here is **honest, weathered, mineral stone** — not a glossy imitation but the quiet, tactile surface of something quarried and worn. It photographs as **calm and grounded**: a palette that moves from the cool serenity of *Loire* to the deep, anchoring *Brionne*, so a whole space can be composed in one stone language across floor, wall and outdoor. The Ancienne Vintage laying leans into a lived-in, old-European-floor narrative; the Noble face reads cleaner and more contemporary. Natural light loves the matte surface.
