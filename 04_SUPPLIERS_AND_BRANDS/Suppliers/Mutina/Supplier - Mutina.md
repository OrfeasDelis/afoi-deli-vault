# Supplier - Mutina

> [!quote]
> An **identity brand** whose moat is **authorship**, not format breadth. Mutina is closer to a furniture or fashion house than a tile factory — every collection is a named, designer-signed object. See [[Afoi Deli — Operating Doctrine#The core asset the uplift engine]].

## What Mutina is to us

The most *art-directed* tile house we carry. Where a commercial factory sells formats and looks, Mutina sells **designers**: Patricia Urquiola, the Bouroullec brothers, Konstantin Grcic, Barber & Osgerby, Inga Sempé, each authoring a signed collection with its own narrative. For a showroom whose whole pitch is taste, Mutina is a pure aesthetic marker — the brand you show an architect to prove the level. Its **Tierras** range ("Mediterranean sedimentation / earth") is the most directly relevant story for our market.

## Product range & collections

> [!info] Source: mutina.it, ingested 2026-06-16. Porcelain stoneware + glazed ceramic + extruded terracotta. ~32 named designer **Tiles** collections plus **Bricks**, **Wood (Accents)** and **Accessories**; per-collection material/colour/format/finish detail and catalogue + technical PDF links live in [[Mutina — Collections Reference]].

**Five categories:** Tiles (the core porcelain-stoneware range), Wall Installations, Bricks (3D extruded terracotta), Wood (oak "Accents", e.g. Pico Bois), Accessories. **Bodies:** unglazed full-body porcelain (Déchirer, Pico, Kosei, Time), glazed rectified porcelain (Mater, Azulej, Mews, Tex, Aurore), glazed pressed ceramic (DIN, Rombini), extruded terracotta (Brac, Tierras Artisanal). **Formats:** small designer modules (DIN 3.6×7.4 → 15×15; Azulej 20×20; Tex 11.5×20) through large slabs **60×60 / 60×120 / 120×120**. **Thicknesses:** ~5–8 mm relief modules, 9–10 mm glazed tiles, 12 mm full-body floors, 16–22 mm Rombini 3D. Mosaics (Botanica, Teknotessere) and 3D wall elements are a signature.

## Distinctive service / strategic angle

1. **Authorship & art direction** — Massimo Orsini's founding intuition (2005): invite world-class product/industrial designers with *no* ceramics background to author tile collections "free from manufacturing tradition." That is the entire moat.
2. **Tierras** — Patricia Urquiola's "Mediterranean memory / earth" range (industrial porcelain on a recycled-ceramic base + artisanal extruded-terracotta Roman bricks). The most spec-relevant story for Greek/Mediterranean premium work.
3. **Cultural platform** — "Mutina for Art" (since 2016), the **Editions** decorative-object line (2021), and immersive **Casa Mutina** living-space showrooms (Modena, Milan) instead of trade booths.
4. **Luxury positioning** — admitted to **Fondazione Altagamma** (Made-in-Italy excellence) as a Design Member in **2024**.

## Company, ownership & sustainability

- **Founded / relaunched:** **2005** by **Massimo Orsini** (CEO & creative lead), who took over a struggling Fiorano ceramics company; trade press dates the underlying business to ~1973. Urquiola joined 2008, the Bouroullecs 2011.
- **Ownership:** privately held, **independent** — no parent ceramics group (unusual among Sassuolo peers). Altagamma membership (2024) is an association, not ownership.
- **Sustainability:** recurring product claim that collections are **V.O.C.-free**; a **Code of Ethics** (2024) and a referenced **Impact Report**. ⚠️ A third-party claim of **LEED Gold / Cradle to Cradle is UNVERIFIED** — do **not** repeat it to clients; formal EPD/ISO certifications are `needs_check`.
