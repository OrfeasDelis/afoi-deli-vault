# Material - Porcelain Stoneware

> [!example] Snapshot
> **Class:** Vitrified ceramic · **AKA:** Gres porcellanato, porcelain tile, full-body/glazed porcelain · **GR:** Γκρες Πορσελάνη / Γκρες Πορσελάνης · **Afoi Deli category:** Tiles · **ISO group:** BIa (Eᵥ ≤ 0.5 %) · **Research depth:** Deep · **Confidence:** verified (material science) with open stock questions flagged below.
> **Key suppliers in our roster:** [[Supplier - Kronos|Kronos]], [[Supplier - Mutina|Mutina]], [[Supplier - Atlas Concorde|Atlas Concorde]], [[Supplier - Florim|Florim]], [[Supplier - Emilgroup|Emilgroup]], [[Supplier - Sant'Agostino|Sant'Agostino]], [[Supplier - ABK|ABK]], [[Supplier - 41zero42|41zero42]] — and most of the tile roster.

The default workhorse of the showroom, and the benchmark every other ceramic is measured against. For the shared classification/spec primer this note leans on, see **[[Afoi Deli — Materials Intelligence#Reading the specs]]**.

## Composition & manufacture

A ceramic body enriched with **fluxes — primarily feldspar** — plus refined clays, kaolin and quartz/sand, coloured with ceramic pigments. The route:

1. Raw materials are **wet-ground** to a slurry, then spray-dried to a fine granulate.
2. The granulate is **pressed under very high pressure** — hydraulic presses exceeding **~500 kg/cm²**.
3. Fired in a roller kiln at **peak temperatures around 1200 °C**, where the **feldspar melts into a viscous glass phase** that fills nearly all the pores. This is **vitrification** — the same process that gives the body its name and its near-zero porosity.

The vitrified microstructure (mullite needles + residual quartz in a glassy matrix) drives **water absorption below 0.5 %** and high mechanical strength (typically **> 40 N/mm²**). That near-zero porosity is also exactly what makes porcelain stoneware **frost-proof, stain-resistant and chemical-resistant** — there is almost no open pore network for water, dirt or ice to enter. A representative body (Florim FloorTech) measures ~**69 % silica, ~21 % alumina**, with minor Ti/Fe/Mg/Ca/Na/K oxides.

## Sub-types — the distinction clients ask about

- **Full-body / technical porcelain** (*gres tecnico*, *tutta massa*, GR: **ολόσωμο**) — colour and structure run **all the way through**. No glaze to wear off, so it outlasts even a glazed PEI 5 tile in punishing traffic. Rated by **deep abrasion (ISO 10545-6)**, not PEI. The honest, raw, architectural look.
- **Glazed porcelain** (*gres smaltato*, GR: **εφυαλωμένο**) — porcelain body with a fired surface glaze carrying the decoration. Vastly wider design range via **digital inkjet printing** — the realistic marble / stone / wood / concrete looks. Rated by **PEI (ISO 10545-7)**.
- **Coloured-body porcelain** — the middle path: the body is tinted close to the surface colour, so chips are far less visible than on a white-body glazed tile.

> [!note] Why this distinction is a sales conversation, not trivia
> "Full-body vs glazed" decides which spec governs (deep-abrasion mm³ vs PEI class) and how the tile ages in heavy traffic. It is the single most useful thing to know about any porcelain line on the floor.

## Formats & finishes

Vast: **30×60, 60×60, 60×120**, 20×20 décor, plank formats for wood-look, up to large slabs (its own engineered form — see [[Material - Large-Format Porcelain Slab|Large-Format Porcelain Slab]]). Thicknesses commonly **8.5–10 mm**, with **20 mm ("2.0")** pavers for outdoor dry-lay / raised floors. Finishes: matte/natural, **polished (lappato/levigato)**, structured, soft/silk, anti-slip (R10–R12), bush-hammered for exteriors.

## Applications

Floors and walls, residential to **heavy commercial**, **indoor and outdoor**, façades, pool surrounds, worktops. The format that goes anywhere — which is why it is the showroom's default and the largest share of the tile roster.

## Care

Effectively maintenance-free; neutral cleaners. **Polished** surfaces can show limescale/etch-like marks from **hard-water Athens** and need more attention than matte — a real, local spec driver worth raising with clients choosing a polished floor.
