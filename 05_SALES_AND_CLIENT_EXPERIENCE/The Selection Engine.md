# The Selection Engine

> [!quote]
> This note descends from [[Afoi Deli — Operating Doctrine]] and, beneath it, [[The Heart]]. The doctrine names the moat as the **uplift engine** and splits it into two halves — **staging** (taste, the room, discretion) and **conversion** (the human gift that makes the client say yes). The staging half is documented across [[Brand DNA]] and the editorial system. **This note is the conversion half**: how, at the sample wall, a general idea becomes a chosen, sold specification. Extracted from a working session on 2026-06-15; revise and enrich freely — nothing here is final.

> [!note] How to read the confidence in this note
> Tags follow [[Vault State Memory|vault convention]] — `verified | likely | memory_seed | needs_check`. Each load-bearing claim is also marked by **source**:
> - **(stated)** — Orfeas said it directly. Treat as fact.
> - **(inferred)** — a read proposed during extraction, *not yet explicitly confirmed*. Treat as a working assumption.
> - **(model)** — a framework we built together from the instances. A conclusion, not a fact.

## 1. The core model — the four-layer selection engine `(model)`

A brief is not selected against directly. It runs through four layers, in order. The first two set the frame; the third defines a *set* of valid answers; the fourth picks the one that actually gets specified.

| # | Layer | What it determines | Example from the Igeiasi case |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Client type** | Which *criteria* the materials must satisfy | Authority specifier → newness, defensibility, feel; price irrelevant |
| 2 | **Room + program** | The **statement hierarchy** — what carries the identity, what stays quiet | Small bath, no shower brassware → the *tile* is the statement; basin built in the same material; fittings stay quiet + value-driven |
| 3 | **Aesthetic target** | An **equivalence class** of materials that "achieve the same result" | "Earthy ceppo with a twist" → Sant'Agostino Gravel ≈ Cielo Le Terre ≈ Galassia *sabbia* |
| 4 | **Constraints** (stock, lead time, budget-fit) | *Which member* of the equivalence class is specified | In-stock / shorter-lead wins over a 6-week factory finish |

> [!important] The load-bearing layer is #3 → #4
> Layer 3 says *what would work*. Layer 4 says *what we'll actually ship*. The expertise lives in holding a wide, accurate equivalence class in your head and then collapsing it against constraints the client never sees. A novice conflates layers 3 and 4 — they either show only what's in stock (no range) or specify the showroom-ideal and get ambushed by lead times.

## 2. The showroom-ideal vs. the specified-real gap `(model)`

> [!tip] The invisible substitution — the signature of the craft
> The first material you *reach for* is the showroom-ideal. The material you *specify* is the constrained-real. The client sees only a complete, confident scheme; they never see the swap.
>
> **Observed live:** first reach was **Cielo Le Acque** (glossy). The constrained-real answer moved to **Cielo Le Terre**, with **Galassia Ceramica *sabbia*** as the more approachable, in-stock, shorter-lead substitute — "achieving the same result with a lesser lead time." `(stated)`

This gap is where margin, stock-utilisation, and delivery reliability are quietly won. It is also the part most worth teaching a new hire, because it is the part they cannot see by watching.

## 3. The two modes of the extreme material `(model — substance stated)`

The same gesture — putting an "extreme" / provocative material in front of a client — does two opposite jobs. **Knowing which mode you are in is the keystone of the whole craft.**

| | **Destination mode** | **Anchor mode** |
|---|---|---|
| Client wants | To be provoked — "something exquisite, even offending their taste" | To be reassured; pleasing, stress-free, value-for-money |
| The extreme piece is | **The sale itself** | A **reference point** — shown on purpose, then stepped down from |
| The move | Hold the extraordinary material with quiet certainty; let it be the answer | Show range, then *guide* down to a grounded choice that now feels both safe and elevated by what it sat beside |
| Failure if misread | Substitute a "grounded" option → you betray exactly what they came for | Push the extreme piece → you lose a client who came to be reassured |

> [!success] Both modes are *staging*, not just selection `(stated)`
> "Selling some craziness sells our brand reach." Whatever actually gets bought, the extreme material has already sold the **range and authority of the house**. This is the [[Afoi Deli — Operating Doctrine|uplift engine]] running straight through the sample wall — the room is doing brand work regardless of the line items.

## 4. Reading the client — the mode-selection tells

This is where layer 1 (client type) is actually *executed*, in the first minutes, before much is shown. Captured reads below; mark each by source.

> [!quote] The verbatim anchor-mode tell `(stated — keep exact)`
> *"We'll keep this in mind for another project of ours, or my own house, for example… **however**…"*
>
> The client is filing the extreme piece under *someday* and pivoting back to real constraints. The **"however" is the pivot**. A novice hears the admiration and pushes the expensive thing; the read is to hear the "however" and bring them down to earth.

### Client-type reads captured so far

> [!info] Authority specifier — e.g. **[[Client - Kaliontzis Fotis|Kaliontzis Fotis]]**
> - **(stated):** status is widely appreciated, taste is looked-up-to, "most clients would do what he says"; **price is irrelevant** — he indicates the *approach*, not the budget.
> - **(inferred — confirm):** criteria are **newness** (reach for the *new* lines — Marazzi *Grande Lume* / *Terramatter* were brand-new), **defensibility** (something he can put his name to in front of his own clients), and selections that **amplify his authority rather than compete with it**.
> - **Mode:** destination.

> [!info] The unsatisfiable villa client — "snobbish wives of rich business owners building their villas"
> - **(stated):** satisfied by nothing. The move is to present a piece (e.g. an **Antonio Lupi** washbasin) with such certainty that *not* appreciating it becomes self-evidently the client's failing — "the same way they wouldn't appreciate an Hermès purse."
> - **Mode:** destination, executed via authority-projection (see §5).

> [!info] The commercial architect — fast, new inquiries
> - **(stated):** fast, to the point, appreciates aesthetics *but* down-to-earth about client needs and construction timelines; wants something pleasing yet **relatively stress-free, different, value-for-money**.
> - **Mode:** anchor (show range, then bring to earth). Self-identifies with the "however" tell above.

> [!warning] These three are the rows we have, not the whole matrix
> Other client types named in [[Business Model Map]] (contractor, developer/construction company, hotelier) were *not* extracted in depth. Their criteria are asserted there as marketing copy, not as real selection rules. **Gap — populate as cases arise.**

## 5. The villa-client authority move — and its danger `(stated + framing)`

> [!quote] The mechanism `(stated)`
> Present the exquisite object with total quiet certainty. You never argue and you are never rude. The gap between the object's *obvious* rightness and the client's hesitation does the work — their uncertainty reads as their deficiency, not as a legitimate preference. (The Hermès analogy is the client's own frame turned back on them.)

This is [[The Heart|"create the circumstances so others ask — never ask"]] applied to a single client at a single wall. It is also, for that reason, **the most dangerous thing in the playbook**:

> [!danger] The dose is everything `(inferred — needs_check)`
> *Hypothesis, not yet confirmed:* there is a line between **daring** the client (rises to it, buys) and **humiliating** them (goes cold, defensive, lost). The same certainty that converts can offend if over-applied. The recovery move when a read goes wrong was **not** extractable in this session — see Open Questions.

## 6. The funnel context — why none of the above does the *qualifying* `(stated + model)`

> [!important] ~80% of walk-ins purchase. There is **no tail follow-up, by design.** `(stated)`
> - No chasing of clients who don't convert. No post-mortem of the ones who walk.
> - This is **doctrine, not laziness**: chasing the 20% would be *asking*, and [[The Heart|"never ask"]] forbids it. The 80% conversion rate is what makes the posture affordable.

> [!note] Consequence for everything above `(model)`
> At an 80% conversion rate, the population at the sample wall is **already filtered** — by reputation, by referral, by *which showroom* they entered ([[Afoi Deli Master Profile|Peristeri vs. Kolonaki]]), by who they are before they speak. So the Selection Engine is doing **conversion, not qualification.** It runs on pre-sorted demand. Any future attempt to "scale selling" (a new hire, the website, an AI at the front of the funnel) must reckon with the fact that the *filter* is currently invisible and upstream of the craft.

## 7. Worked example — Kaliontzis Fotis / Igeiasi offices `(stated — preserve in full)`

**Brief:** office bathrooms (tile + sanitary ware). Client gave **two guideline routes**, not a spec:
- **A —** earthy, *ceppo*-style, "with a twist."
- **B —** a full 70s colourful approach.

**Both routes closed into complete schemes on the spot:**

| | Route A (earthy ceppo + twist) | Route B (70s) |
|---|---|---|
| **Tile (the statement)** | **Sant'Agostino Gravel** — digital tech, near-real tactility *(first reach)* → constrained-real: **Cielo Le Terre** or **Galassia *sabbia*** (in-stock, shorter lead) | **Marazzi *Grande Lume*** + **Terramatter** (new, strong 70s aesthetic) |
| **Sanitary ware** | Built-in countertop washbasins **in the same material as the tile**; value-for-money Italian collection, glossy, in stock | (same logic — fittings stay quiet, tile carries it) |
| **Brassware** | Discreet **Fima Carlo Frattini *Snap*** | (same) |

**The constraint logic the client never saw `(stated)`:** small rooms + **no shower faucets** → no case for heavy brassware investment; use **stock** items; avoid finishes with **~6-week factory import lead times**. The fittings were chosen value-first *because the room couldn't justify otherwise and the tile was the statement* — not as a fixed "elegant substrate."

> [!tip] Sub-pattern parked for later: hearing "with a twist" → **tactility** `(inferred)`
> The answer to "a twist" was a *tactile* surprise — Gravel's hyperrealism, a ceppo that surprises the hand — rather than a colour or pattern move. Possible general rule: certain brief-words map to certain material *dimensions*. Worth testing across more cases before treating as doctrine.

## 8. Open questions / to verify

> [!question] Resolve as cases arise — do not fill with guesses
> 1. **Who are the 20%?** The clients Afoi Deli is content to let walk — price-shopper, haggler, friction-with-no-margin — vs. a number worth moving. *Refused/declined in session; genuinely open.* This determines whether "no follow-up" is a floor or a ceiling.
> 2. **Confirm the Kaliontzis criteria** (newness / defensibility / amplify-authority) — proposed, not confirmed.
> 3. **The villa-client recovery move** — what the tell is when authority-projection isn't landing, and how to pull back without losing the sale *or* surrendering the authority. (See §5 danger.)
> 4. **The "twist → tactility" rule** — confirm or refute across more briefs (§7).
> 5. **The missing matrix rows** — contractor, developer/construction company, hotelier: real selection criteria, not the [[Business Model Map]] marketing version.
